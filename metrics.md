# Audio-Only Communication Assessment Rubric

Six independent agents evaluate distinct dimensions of spoken communication from audio input. Each agent scores independently on a 1–5 scale, anchored to observable audio evidence.

---

## 1. Delivery & Clarity

**What it listens for:** everything about how the voice physically produces sound.

Pace, volume, pitch variation, prosody, strategic pauses, articulation crispness, and pronunciation accuracy. A high score means the voice is both engaging (varied, well-modulated) and clear (every word intelligible, terms pronounced correctly). Does not judge what is said or how smoothly — only the acoustic output.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| **5** | Dynamic pitch range and deliberate pace variation; pauses strategic and well-placed; every word crisply articulated; technical terms and proper nouns pronounced accurately; voice itself draws the listener in. |
| **4** | Noticeable variety in pitch, pace, and volume; clearly intelligible throughout; one or two minor slurs or mispronunciations; occasional flat stretches but overall engaging. |
| **3** | Some vocal variation but largely predictable; pace acceptable (roughly 120–170 wpm); generally intelligible with occasional mumbled words or mispronounced terms; neither dull nor compelling. |
| **2** | Mostly monotone or rushed/dragging; few meaningful pauses; frequent mumbling or dropped consonants; listener loses words several times per minute. |
| **1** | Flat monotone or erratic pacing throughout; volume problems interfere with listening; persistently unclear articulation; multiple words unintelligible per utterance. |

---

## 2. Fluency

**What it listens for:** smoothness and continuity of speech flow.

Filler word frequency, false starts, self-corrections, hesitations, repetitions, trailing-off sentences. A high score means speech flows without friction and thoughts complete cleanly. Independent of delivery — a fluent speaker can still be monotone, and an expressive speaker can still stumble.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| **5** | Near-zero fillers (under ~2 per minute); thoughts complete cleanly; no audible self-correction or false starts. |
| **4** | Low filler rate (~2–5/min); occasional self-correction handled smoothly; minor hesitations but forward momentum intact. |
| **3** | Noticeable fillers (~6–10/min); some false starts and restarts; flow is functional but uneven. |
| **2** | Frequent fillers (~11–15/min); regular hesitations, restarts, or trailing-off sentences; choppy feel. |
| **1** | Fillers exceed ~15/min or speech is dominated by hesitations, abandoned sentences, and restarts; flow is broken. |

---

## 3. Language Precision

**What it listens for:** word choice and linguistic construction.

Vocabulary range and accuracy, grammatical correctness, syntactic variety, register appropriateness, conciseness vs. padding. A high score means words are chosen deliberately and sentences are well-formed. Does not evaluate organization — only whether the language itself is sound.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| **5** | Precise, varied vocabulary; grammatically clean; sentence structure varied and well-matched to register; zero padding. |
| **4** | Accurate word choice with occasional vivid phrasing; minor grammar slips; good register match; little wasted language. |
| **3** | Generally correct grammar and vocabulary; some repetition or imprecise word choices; register mostly appropriate. |
| **2** | Frequent grammatical errors, vague wording, or register mismatch; noticeable padding or vague qualifiers. |
| **1** | Persistent grammar problems, incorrect word choices, or register wildly inappropriate; meaning often obscured by language issues. |

---

## 4. Structure & Coherence

**What it listens for:** organization of ideas at the discourse level.

Clear opening, logical progression, verbal signposting, coherent transitions, strength of reasoning, meaningful closure. A high score means a listener could mentally outline the response after hearing it once. Independent of language quality — beautiful sentences in random order fail here.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| **5** | Clear opening, logical progression, explicit signposting, strong conclusion; listener could outline the response from memory. |
| **4** | Organized with identifiable sections; most transitions work; minor coherence gaps; clear enough to follow without effort. |
| **3** | Main points discernible but order is loose; transitions sometimes missing; conclusion weak or abrupt; listener can follow with effort. |
| **2** | Ideas present but poorly sequenced; few transitions; points overlap or repeat; no clear beginning-middle-end. |
| **1** | Stream-of-consciousness or chaotic; no visible structure; listener cannot identify main points or progression. |

---

## 5. Engagement & Rapport

**What it listens for:** interpersonal and audience-aware qualities audible in the audio.

Warmth, enthusiasm, confidence, empathy cues, responsiveness to the other speaker, turn-taking quality, inclusive language, adaptation to the listener. A high score means the speaker sounds *connected*, not just broadcasting. Separate from vocal delivery — a technically varied voice can still feel cold.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| **5** | Voice conveys warmth, confidence, and attunement; in dialogue, strong listening cues, reflective acknowledgments, and adaptive tone shifts; sounds genuinely connected to the audience. |
| **4** | Generally warm and confident; responsive to context; occasional flat moments but clearly audience-aware. |
| **3** | Neutral tone; neither cold nor notably warm; in dialogue, basic turn-taking without much attunement; broadcasts more than connects. |
| **2** | Cold, hesitant, or over-formal; minimal responsiveness in dialogue; interruptions, mistimed turns, or tone-deaf moments. |
| **1** | Detached, dismissive, anxious, or hostile in tone; no evident audience awareness; interpersonally jarring. |

---

## 6. Subject Matter Expertise

**What it listens for:** how deeply the speaker demonstrates understanding of the topic being discussed.

Accuracy of claims, depth of knowledge beyond surface-level facts, appropriate use of domain terminology, ability to explain mechanisms and causes rather than just state conclusions, handling of nuance and edge cases, responsiveness to follow-up or challenging questions, acknowledgment of limits or uncertainty where warranted, and ability to draw connections across related concepts. A high score means the speaker sounds like someone who *actually knows* the subject, not someone reciting or improvising. Independent of how well they *say* it — a deep expert can be monotone and scattered, and a polished speaker can be hollow.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| **5** | Demonstrates deep, well-integrated knowledge; uses domain terminology precisely and appropriately; explains mechanisms, not just conclusions; handles nuance, edge cases, and follow-ups with ease; acknowledges genuine uncertainty where warranted; draws non-obvious connections. |
| **4** | Solid command of the subject; accurate claims with mostly correct terminology; can explain the "why" behind most points; handles standard follow-ups well; occasional surface-level moments but no meaningful errors. |
| **3** | Generally accurate on the basics; terminology used correctly but sometimes imprecisely; explanations tend to stay at the "what" level rather than the "why"; handles simple follow-ups but falters on depth or nuance. |
| **2** | Notable gaps or inaccuracies; terminology misused or avoided; explanations feel rehearsed or surface-level; struggles with follow-up questions; bluffs through nuance rather than engaging with it. |
| **1** | Frequent factual errors, misused terminology, or clear misunderstanding of core concepts; unable to go beyond talking points; follow-ups expose confusion; speaker sounds out of their depth. |

---

## Scoring Guidance

- **Score each agent independently.** Ignore the other dimensions. A monotone voice with perfect grammar gets Vocal Delivery 2 and Language Precision 5.
- **Anchor every score to specific audio evidence.** Cite moments or patterns, not overall impression.
- **Default to 3** when evidence is genuinely mixed or ambiguous.
- **Scale expectations for short clips.** For audio under ~30 seconds, scale fluency counts and structural expectations to the format.
- **Watch for divergent combinations.** High expertise with low delivery, or high fluency with low expertise, are meaningful signals — not scoring errors.

## Agent Output Format

Each agent should return:

- **Score** (1–5)
- **Evidence** — two or three specific audio observations justifying the score
- **Improvement** — one concrete suggestion for moving up one level
